Supporting the Establishment of the Belize Recycling and Waste Management Association
Read More WREN takes pride in leading the establishment of the "Belize Recycling and Waste Management Association," a groundbreaking initiative as the first of its kind in the Caribbean Through a consultative and participatory process, including targeted consultations and workshops, our dedicated team aims to address the lack of a unified platform for informal recyclers and private sector waste managers. The ultimate goal is to strengthen collaboration between the private sector and the Belizean government in enhancing resource recovery. Once formed, the association will continue to receive support through engagement with national and regional counterparts and hosting a donor round table. Read Less
Developing a National Maritime Transport Policy for Belize
Read More Supported by the Centre for Environment Fisheries and Aquaculture Science (Cefas, UK) and funded through the UK Government’s Ocean Country Partnership Program (OCPP), WREN is actively leading the development of a comprehensive Maritime Transport Policy for Belize. This initiative recognizes the significant contributions of various maritime sectors to Belize's development, including tourism, passenger transport, shipping, and emerging industries. Emphasizing sustainability, connectivity, and resilience, our team is working towards producing a National Maritime Transport Policy that provides strategic direction. Read Less
Conducting a Pilot Ports Upgrade Feasibility Study on Port Waste Management Infrastructure in Belize
Read More WREN is delivering a focused feasibility study on upgrading port waste management infrastructure in Belize. Centered around the maritime sector, this study assesses the feasibility of port facility and system upgrades to align with the International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships (MARPOL). Our project involves a comprehensive analysis of existing maritime sector activities and port facility operations, evaluating infrastructure requirements for MARPOL compliance. Read Less
Project Fresh Start – Introducing collection services in remote communities in Belize
Read More Addressing the issue of illegal garbage dumping, Project Fresh Start aims to establish waste collection and disposal systems in small and remote communities in Belize that did not previously have existing waste management infrastructure. WREN collaborates with the Department of Environment (DOE) and village councils to create effective waste management systems, mitigating environmental and health issues associated with illegal dumping. White Paper - Compendium of Successful Case Studies on Effective Solutions to Plastic Pollution in Islands
Read More WREN addresses plastic pollution in Small Island Developing States (SIDs) through a white paper consolidating successful case studies. Assessment on Preventing Leakage of Single-Use Plastics in Pacific Island Countries
Read More In collaboration with an international team of experts, WREN’s director is contributing to an assessment to prevent the leakage of single-use plastics into the marine environment in Pacific Island Countries (PICs). Targeting Fiji, Kiribati, Samoa, and Vanuatu, the project aims to provide these governments with a framework to reduce single-use plastic leakage. Through a comprehensive cost-benefit analysis, considering economic, social, and environmental factors, the study will inform decision-making, policies, and investment planning for sustainable production and consumption of plastics. Assessment of the Scope, Scale, and Impacts of Marine Pollution on Key Blue Economy Sectors in Pacific Island Countries
Read More Working as part of an international collaborative team, WREN’s director is contributing as the solid waste management expert for the "Assessment of the Scope, Scale, and Impacts of Marine Pollution from Key Blue Economy Sectors in 12 PICs." The Pacific Ocean Litter Project (POLP)
Read More Working as part of an international collaborative The Pacific Ocean Litter Project (POLP) (2019-2027) is funded by the Australian Government and implemented by the Secretariat of the Pacific Regional Environment Programme (SPREP) in collaboration with Pacific Island Countries (PICs). The POLP is working to reduce single-use plastic marine litter in the coastal environments of PICs. It has been developed in recognition of the threat marine litter poses to the environment, public health, and economic development of the Pacific region. There is a pressing need to consider alternative solutions to single-use plastic products. Under this preoject, WREN will produce a region-wide compendium of alternative products that are being used in the region and globally to replace single-use plastics that are targeted by the POLP.

The Activity will provide the foundation to progress towards the following intermediate outcomes:

  • Pacific Islander owned businesses and community-based enterprises are making alternative sustainable products locally
  • SPREP is sharing information and tools that enable governments, industry, and business to choose viable alternative sustainable products – imported or produced locally.
    • Alternative products identified in the compendium will be considered for adoption through pilot activities in one or more Pacific Island countries. To support POLP outcomes, the compendium and assessment will identify recommended suppliers of alternative products and potential opportunities to produce alternative products in PICs.

    Countries: All PICs

    Client: SPREP
